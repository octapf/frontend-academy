# Redux Toolkit: the idea and when to use it

## What you’ll take away

**One state tree** updated by **predictable actions**: useful when many screens share business rules or you need traceability (time-travel, logs).

## Details

Redux Toolkit (`configureStore`, `createSlice`) cuts repetition vs hand-written Redux. It is not mandatory everywhere: it competes with **Context + reducers**, **Zustand**, or **server state** (TanStack Query). Interviews care about **justifying cost** (boilerplate, learning curve) vs **benefit** (consistency in large teams, middleware).

## Recommended practice

- List **three sources of truth** in your app (UI, server, URL) and decide what belongs in Redux vs network cache.
- Open a real `slice` and trace **UI → dispatch → reducer → selector**.

## Common pitfalls

- Putting **everything** in the store (including ephemeral form state nobody else needs).
- Selectors that return **fresh objects** every render and break downstream memoization.
