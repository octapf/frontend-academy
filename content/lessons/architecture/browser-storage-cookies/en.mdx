# Cookies, localStorage, and sessionStorage

## What you’ll take away

**Cookies** ride HTTP requests (good for server-side session, `HttpOnly`/`Secure`/`SameSite`). **localStorage** persists per origin until cleared. **sessionStorage** lasts for the tab only.

## Details

They do not replace each other: cookies enable browser policies storage cannot; storage avoids header size on every request but **is not for secrets** (any JS on the origin can read it). Neighbors: **CSRF** on session cookies, **XSS** stealing storage, **GDPR** if you store identifiers.

## Recommended practice

- For a session token, reason through **HttpOnly cookie** vs **memory** vs **storage** given your threat model.
- List what your app stores today and **who** can read it (JS, server, extensions).

## Common pitfalls

- Storing **JWTs** in localStorage “because it is easy” without sizing XSS exposure.
- Assuming `sessionStorage` is “secure” — it only isolates per tab; it does not authenticate.
