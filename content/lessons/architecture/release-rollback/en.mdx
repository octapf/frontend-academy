# Rollback without drama

**Rollback** returns to a prior version when a release hurts metrics or introduces bugs. In modern frontend it is rarely “flip DNS by hand”: it is usually **re-deploying** a known artifact or **toggling** the change off.

## Canary and percentage

Shipping to a **subset** of users or regions shrinks the blast radius. Pair with observability and alerts.

## Communication

In the release channel document **what to watch**, **who is on call**, and **how long** you wait before deciding rollback. Long silence increases incident cost.

## Link

**Feature flags** and **Observability** are the usual levers to operate controlled releases.
