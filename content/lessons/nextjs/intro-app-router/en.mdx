# Next.js: App Router on one page

## What you’ll take away

How `app/`, `page.tsx`, `layout.tsx`, and **`'use client'`** split responsibilities in modern apps.

## Details

In the **App Router** URLs follow folders; components are **Server Components** by default (less client JS, `fetch` near data). When you need local state or DOM events, isolate **`'use client'`** in small leaves. Neighbors: **metadata**, **Route Handlers**, **`fetch` caching**, **middleware**, and env vars — each tackles a different risk (SEO, secrets, redirects).

## Recommended practice

- Sketch **app/** tree → **URLs** for your project or an official example.
- Pick a client component and ask whether it **could be server** by lifting data.

## Common pitfalls

- Putting `'use client'` at the root “just in case” and shipping the whole subtree in the browser bundle.
- Expecting both **`page.tsx` and `route.ts`** in the same segment to serve two different things for one URL.
