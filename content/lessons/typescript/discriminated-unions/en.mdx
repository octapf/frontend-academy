# Discriminated unions

A **discriminated union** is a union where each variant shares a literal field (for example `kind`).

## Why it matters

- Cleaner control flow with `switch`
- Prevent impossible states
- Enforce exhaustiveness (missing a case becomes a TypeScript error)

In the exercise you’ll compute areas for shapes using `kind: "circle" | "rect"`.
