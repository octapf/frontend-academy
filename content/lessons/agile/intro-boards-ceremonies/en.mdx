# Practical agile: boards and ceremonies

## What you’ll take away

**Refined backlog**, **short sprint**, and **Definition of Done** as tools to reduce surprises — not bureaucracy.

## Details

In real teams the board (Jira, Linear, etc.) links **product priority** with **technical risk**: spikes, dependencies, debt. For interviews it helps to describe **how you ask for clarity** on a story (“what is acceptance?”, “what is the success metric?”) and **how you negotiate scope** when design is not final.

## Recommended practice

- Write **Definition of Done** for a typical UI change (a11y, telemetry, tests).
- Take a vague ticket and rewrite it as **verifiable criteria**.

## Common pitfalls

- Treating **velocity** as individual productivity.
- Assuming “agile” means no documentation or design.
